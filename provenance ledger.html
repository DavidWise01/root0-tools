What this tool is for

A personal provenance ledger for tracking your own intellectual work. It records what you made and when you logged it, with cryptographic hashes that prove the content hasn't been altered since logging. That's the whole tool.

What this tool is not for

This isn't a litigation tool. It isn't designed for asserting claims against other parties. It isn't a system for tracking what other people might owe you. Honest provenance is about your relationship to your own work, not about leveraging that relationship against anyone else.

Why this matters

Strong provenance records protect you in legitimate situations: showing you authored something before someone else's claim, demonstrating which version of a draft was current at a given moment, tracking how your own ideas evolved. They don't manufacture grounds for disputes that wouldn't exist otherwise.

Honest principles

Hashes prove content, not creation. A SHA-256 hash proves a particular bit-string existed at a particular moment when you logged it. It doesn't prove you originated the underlying ideas — only that you recorded a particular formulation of them.
Local timestamps reflect your clock. Your computer's clock isn't authoritative. For stronger provenance, supplement this with public time-stamping services (OpenTimestamps, blockchain anchoring, or just emailing yourself a copy through a third-party service).
Provenance ≠ priority. Recording your work first doesn't mean you originated the idea. Independent discovery is common in active fields. Treat this ledger as a record of your trajectory, not a claim against others.
Knowledge over money. The best use of this tool is helping you track how your own thinking develops. Lineage trees let you see your own intellectual evolution. That's the value of the ledger, not its utility in disputes.
Tools shape behavior. A tool framed as "track your work" produces different downstream behavior than one framed as "build legal case." This one is the first kind. If your needs change, the design of the tool would need to change too — and that change is a deliberate choice, not a default.
