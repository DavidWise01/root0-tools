How the reactivity works

Energy enters the shell when you seed it. Energy decays over each cycle by the decay rate. The bridges respond to current energy level:

High energy (above 60%): bridges fully ground. The accumulator zeros at each bridge crossing. Walker burrows the cycle with reset between halves. This is burrow behavior.

Medium energy (20–60%): bridges partially ground. Some accumulator phase carries through. The shell starts collecting at hemisphere nodes. This is gather behavior emerging.

Low energy (below 20%): bridges no longer ground. Accumulator phase persists. Walker drops the cycle and rests at the seed. This is protect behavior — the seed is preserved against further activity.

The three phases aren't modes you switch between. They emerge from energy level. Seed high → burrow. Seed mid → gather. Seed depleted → protect. Re-seed restarts the cycle.

The four hemisphere operations on the accumulator

The walker carries an accumulator (a phase value in [-1, +1]). Each hemisphere node transforms it:

−1 at the seed: negate. accum = −accum. Inversion.

−i: rotate −90°. accum = −1 − accum (or rotation in 2D phase space). Pulls toward the negative.

+1: identity. accum = accum. Pass-through.

+i: rotate +90°. accum = +1 − accum. Pulls toward the positive.

Bridge nodes (the zeros) react: accum = accum × bridgeGrounding where bridgeGrounding ranges from 1 (full ground) at high energy to 0 (no ground) at low energy. This is the reactive piece — bridges aren't always zero, they're zero when there's enough energy to ground them.

What this shell does as a unit

Seed in → walker runs → bridges respond to remaining energy → output gathered at hemisphere nodes when energy drops into gather range → final state preserved at seed when energy depletes.

Each cycle (8 steps) is one pass. Many cycles per seed. Each cycle costs energy at the rate set by the decay slider. When seeded with strength 80% and decay 6, you typically get 2–3 burrow cycles, 3–4 gather cycles, then settling.

The gathered output (the bottom card) is the shell's product. In a scaled system, this becomes the seed for a higher-level shell. Base-8 scaling: 8 shells produce 8 gathered values, those 8 values seed one next-level shell.
